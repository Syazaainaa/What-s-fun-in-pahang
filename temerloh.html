NATIONAL ELEPHANT CONSERVATION CENTER

Fun Times with majestic creatures in Kuala Gandah Jan 18, 2022

The museum displays many artifacts that are important in the history of the country and local Malay culture. The museum was officially opened by Sultan Ahmad Shah around October 1976. This interesting place in Pahang Town is located in the former residence of the British Resident, built in 1929. The muzuim was later bought by the Sultan of Pahang in 1948 and was renamed as Kota Beram Palace. In 1965, the Sultan moved his residence to a new palace (Abu Bakar Palace), where he was the royal palace now. By visiting this muzuim, you can learn a variety of interesting Islamic arts of the past as there are various galleries, artifacts, ceramics, and archeology on display.

TEMERLOH DAY MARKET

The largest market in peninsular Malaysia Jan 24, 2022

Pekan Sehari, Bandar Temerloh is the largest in peninsular Malaysia located on the shores of Sungai Temerloh along more than 2,000 meters involving almost 1,000 traders to be the focus of the community to get various goods. Located near Sungai Pahang in Jalan Lurah Semantan, Temerloh Day Market is packed with sales of various types of goods, these include clothes, souvenirs, food, wet ingredients and more.

"Ikan Patin" City

Temerloh is a developed and organized city. It is located in Central Pahang and is the meeting point between the West Coast of Peninsular Malaysia and the East Coast of Peninsular Malaysia. Temerloh is also known as Bandar Ikan Patin because this type of fish is abundant here, as the city is close to the river better known as Sungai Pahang.
