SULTAN ABU BAKAR MUSEUM

Historical residence of the Sultan of Pahang with many permanent exhibitions Jan 16, 2022

The museum displays many artifacts that are important in the history of the country and local Malay culture. The museum was officially opened by Sultan Ahmad Shah around October 1976. This interesting place in Pahang Town is located in the former residence of the British Resident, built in 1929. The muzuim was later bought by the Sultan of Pahang in 1948 and was renamed as Kota Beram Palace. In 1965, the Sultan moved his residence to a new palace (Abu Bakar Palace), where he was the royal palace now. By visiting this muzuim, you can learn a variety of interesting Islamic arts of the past as there are various galleries, artifacts, ceramics, and archeology on display.

SULTAN ABDULLAH MOSQUE MUSEUM

Historical mosque is now a museum focused on Islamic art and history Jan 27, 2022

The mosque is known among locals as the Taj Mahal Malaysia because of its architectural design that is similar to the Taj Mahal. The Mosque Museum was opened around the middle of 2015 for public visits and has the concept of a museum in the park. The gallery is provided for visitors to learn about various Islamic events in the past at this interesting place in Pekan Pahang. Royal town

Pekan Town is a town in Pekan District, Pahang, Malaysia. It is also the royal town of the state. Its name comes from a flower, the Bunga Pekan. Pekan is also the name of the district the town is situated in, and a parliamentary constituency in its own right
