PAHANG DARUL MAKMUR

Pahang is the largest state in peninsular Malaysia Jan 10, 2022

Pahang Darul Makmur is Malaysia's biggest state, borders Kelantan to the north, Perak, Selangor, and Negeri Sembilan to the west, Johor to the south, and Terengganu and the South China Sea to the east.

The state of Pahang covers an area of 35,965 square kilometers (km2) divided into 11 administrative districts, namely Pekan, Kuantan, Rompin, Maran, Bera, Jerantut, Temerloh, Raub, Bentong, Lipis and Cameron Highlands.

Pahang River

Longest river in Peningsular Malaysia Jan 23, 2022

Pahang River is the longest river in peninsular Malaysia and 2/3 of the state of Pahang is in the Pahang river basin. Two districts outside Pahang, namely Jelebu and part of Jermpol district in Negeri Sembilan are also in the Pahang basin. A stretch of 285 miles from the Titiwangsa Mountains to the South China Sea is the Pahang River. The river was important for navigation in the Middle Ages, connecting the east and west coasts of the Malay Peninsula between the 15th and 16th centuries. The settlers then utilized the river water for use in coconut and rubber plantations. Rivers in modern Malaysia are critical for water supply not only in the State of Pahang but all other states located in Peninsular Malaysia. The river is also used by the community in addition to the banks for fishing and agriculture. Widespread deforestation has led to massive floods during the monsoon season.
